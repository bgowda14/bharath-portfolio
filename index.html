About Me.

I am currently employed at Arizona State University as a Financial Data Operations Assistant, where I manage payment workflows, assist with financial reconciliation, and support backend systems to ensure data accuracy and operational efficiency. This role has strengthened my analytical thinking, attention to detail, and understanding of enterprise-level financial systems.

In addition to my professional experience, I served as the Prototype Lead for a research initiative focused on developing a wearable device that translates American Sign Language into audible speech in real-time. The project combined embedded systems, sensor calibration, and real-time data processing, and it achieved over 90% accuracy during testing. I also independently developed a motion-detecting security system using Python and OpenCV, which turns any webcam into a live CCTV system with real-time alert capabilities — currently available on my GitHub.

I am pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science with a 3.8 GPA and a certificate in Business Data Analytics. My academic and project experience spans across software development, data analysis, cybersecurity, and embedded systems. I am passionate about building impactful, practical technology solutions that blend technical innovation with user-centered design.
